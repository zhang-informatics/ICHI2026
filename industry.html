Evaluating Large Language Models for Clinical Decision Support

A policy-focused keynote on AI metrology, trustworthiness, and rigorous evaluation standards for clinical LLMs.

Speaker

Ram D. Sriram, PhD

Senior Scientific Advisor, Information Technology Laboratory

National Institute of Standards and Technology

Moderator

Ikram Khan

Managing Director

Health AI Institute

Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly being used by the healthcare profession to support clinical decision-making through analysis of unstructured text, medical literature, and clinical guidance. As these systems move closer to real-world deployment, the need for rigorous measurement and evaluation becomes unavoidable.

This keynote introduces the emerging field of AI metrology and outlines why standardized methods are needed to assess the accuracy, reliability, and trustworthiness of healthcare LLMs. It reviews evaluation approaches for healthcare chatbots from an end-user perspective, including language understanding, clinical task performance, and conversational effectiveness.

The session also looks ahead to agentic AI and the additional metrics that will be required to evaluate these more autonomous healthcare architectures responsibly.

From Data to Policy: Governing AI and Health Informatics in the Age of Intelligent Care

A cross-sector discussion on accountability, privacy, equity, and governance for health AI and real-world data.

Overview

The rapid integration of artificial intelligence, large-scale health data systems, and interoperable informatics platforms into clinical and public health settings has outpaced the policy frameworks designed to govern them.

This panel convenes leaders from industry, government, and standards bodies to examine where innovation meets regulation, including AI accountability in clinical decision support, data privacy under evolving federal and state frameworks, health equity in algorithmic systems, and governance of real-world data for drug and device evaluation.

Discussion Themes

  • Policy pathways that protect patients without stalling innovation
  • Governance of clinical AI and real-world data infrastructures

10:00 AM - 11:30 AM Panel Discussion
Panel Discussion Theater

The GenAI Data Chasm: Are We Optimizing Models for Patients, Providers, or Businesses, or Are We Truly Optimizing Care?

A practical discussion on whether healthcare AI is being built around clinically meaningful, human-aligned data ecosystems or around deeper, more intelligent silos.

Overview

The GenAI data chasm highlights a critical barrier to healthcare AI adoption: the gap between simply having data and having data that is trustworthy, representative, and fit for medical-grade systems. While generative AI promises to transform diagnostics, drug discovery, and patient care, that progress depends on building a robust and integrated data ecosystem.

Patient journeys are segmented across conditions, care settings, modalities, and time. This panel examines how healthcare organizations can move beyond generalized datasets toward segment-aware strategies that capture clinically meaningful variation and connect multimodal data to longitudinal outcomes.

The discussion also looks at how experts increasingly contribute structured rationale and uncertainty, and how emerging evaluation approaches can better support reinforcement learning and agentic AI in healthcare workflows.

Discussion Focus

  • Trustworthy, representative, and fit-for-purpose healthcare data
  • Human-aligned data ecosystems versus deeper AI silos
  • Evaluation strategies for reinforcement learning and agentic AI

11:45 AM - 12:45 PM Conference Keynote
Conference Keynote Theater

Towards Virtual Patient: AI for Accelerating Medical Discovery

A conference keynote on multimodal generative AI, digital twins, and virtual patient models for real-world evidence.

Speaker

Hoifung Poon, PhD

General Manager

Microsoft Research, USA

Abstract

Today, medical discovery advances one clinical trial at a time, each taking years to execute and often costing $100 million or more. As we enter the era of precision health, where treatments are increasingly tailored to individuals, continuing with current discovery processes is not sustainable.

The rapid digitization of multimodal longitudinal patient journeys, including electronic health records, imaging, and multiomics, opens the door to a new research agenda: advancing multimodal generative AI that can learn the language of patients and create virtual patient world models as digital twins for forecasting disease progression and treatment response.

This approach enables synthesis of population-scale real-world evidence from hundreds of millions of patients and has the potential to accelerate medical discovery through AI-powered virtual clinical trials.

3:20 PM - 4:20 PM Closing Keynote
Closing Keynote Theater

From Algorithm to Adoption: Evolving BeatLogic into Trusted Real-World AI for Cardiac Monitoring

A closing keynote and Q&A on what it takes to move from a strong model to a trusted operational AI capability in healthcare.

Speaker

Ben Teplitzky, PhD

AI/ML Research Fellow, Patient Management

Boston Scientific, USA

Abstract

Artificial intelligence in healthcare is often presented through model performance, but real-world impact depends on much more than accuracy alone. In ambulatory cardiac monitoring, the challenge is not simply detecting arrhythmias from ECG signals; it is building AI that can operate reliably across noisy, heterogeneous, real-world data, fit into clinical workflows, support expert review, and earn trust from clinicians, patients, and health systems.

In this keynote, Ben Teplitzky traces the evolution of BeatLogic from an early deep-learning platform for comprehensive ECG annotation to a broader case study in healthcare AI enablement. Early work showed that combining deep learning with high-quality expert-adjudicated data could substantially improve beat and rhythm interpretation in ambulatory monitoring, but translating AI into practice required confronting the harder informatics problems of dataset design, annotation quality, edge-case discovery, validation strategy, workflow integration, human oversight, and continuous iteration.

The session offers a pragmatic roadmap for creating human-centered AI that is safe, scalable, and genuinely useful in healthcare, supported by a closing Q&A discussion.
