8:15 AM - 8:35 AM Policy Keynote
Policy Keynote

Evaluating Large Language Models for Clinical Decision Support

Ram D. Sriram, PhD | Senior Science Advisor, Information Technology Laboratory, National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), USA

Moderator: Ikram Khan, Health AI Institute

Abstract: Large language models are increasingly used to support clinicians by analyzing unstructured text, medical literature, and practice guidelines. This keynote examines the growing need for rigorous AI metrology in healthcare, with a focus on standardized ways to measure the accuracy, reliability, and trustworthiness of clinical LLMs. The talk also reviews emerging evaluation metrics for healthcare chatbots from an end-user perspective, including language understanding, clinical task performance, conversational effectiveness, and the additional measurement challenges introduced by agentic AI systems.

8:35 AM - 9:45 AM Policy Panel
Policy Panel

From Data to Policy: Governing AI and Health Informatics in the Age of Intelligent Care

A cross-sector discussion on policy, accountability, privacy, equity, and real-world data governance.

Panel overview: The rapid integration of artificial intelligence, large-scale health data systems, and interoperable informatics platforms into clinical and public health settings has outpaced the policy frameworks designed to govern them. This panel convenes leaders from industry, government, and standards bodies to examine where innovation meets regulation, including AI accountability in clinical decision support, data privacy under evolving federal and state frameworks, health equity in algorithmic systems, and the governance of real-world data for drug and device evaluation.

The discussion is designed to surface practical pathways for policy that both enable innovation and protect patients, while inviting the ICHI community to consider how researchers and technologists can actively shape the regulatory landscape instead of only reacting to it.

10:00 AM - 11:30 AM Panel Discussion
Panel Discussion

The GenAI Data Chasm: Are we building a Human-Aligned Unified Data Ecosystem or Just Deeper, More Intelligent Silos?

Success stories, lessons learned, technical challenges, and practical solutions in applied healthcare AI.

Panel overview: This session focuses on one of the biggest barriers to successful AI adoption in healthcare: the gap between simply having data and having data that is trustworthy, representative, and fit for purpose in a medical-grade AI ecosystem. The discussion examines how generative AI ambitions in diagnostics, drug discovery, patient care, and smart medical devices depend on building a more robust and unified data foundation.

Panelists will explore whether the field is moving toward safe, scalable, human-aligned data ecosystems or creating deeper and more intelligent silos that trap value, amplify bias, and weaken real-world impact for patients, providers, and innovators.

Discussion Focus

Moderator: Prasanna Desikan, Centific

  • Trustworthy and representative real-world data
  • Unified infrastructure versus fragmented AI silos
  • Bias, safety, and long-term scalability
11:45 AM - 12:45 PM Conference Keynote
Conference Keynote

Towards Virtual Patient: AI for Accelerating Medical Discovery

Hoifung Poon, PhD | General Manager, Microsoft Research, USA

Abstract: Today, medical discovery advances one clinical trial at a time, often taking years and costing more than $100 million per study. As precision health moves beyond one-size-fits-all care, this keynote explores how multimodal generative AI can learn the language of patients from longitudinal clinical, imaging, and multi-omics data to create a virtual patient world model. Such digital twins could synthesize population-scale real-world evidence from hundreds of millions of patients and accelerate medical discovery through AI-powered virtual clinical trials.

Panel Discussion

AI Enablement in Practice: Exploring Strategies, Enablers, and Pitfalls of AI Adoption

Public-private partnerships and industry-academia collaboration in real-world deployment.

Panel overview: As artificial intelligence matures from research prototypes to real-world clinical deployment, healthcare leaders face hard questions about how to scale adoption safely and effectively. This panel examines lessons learned from AI implementations across industry, academia, and care delivery organizations, with attention to workflow integration, model lifecycle management, regulatory expectations, bias mitigation, clinician trust, and patient trust.

The session emphasizes practical enablers, recurring pitfalls, and actionable approaches for organizations working toward responsible and equitable AI integration.

3:20 PM - 4:20 PM Industry Keynote
Industry Keynote

From Algorithm to Adoption: Evolving BeatLogic into Trusted Real-World AI for Cardiac Monitoring

Ben Teplitzky, PhD | AI/ML Research Fellow, Patient Management, Boston Scientific, USA

Abstract: Artificial intelligence in healthcare is often presented through model performance, but real-world impact depends on much more than accuracy alone. Using BeatLogic as a case study, this keynote traces how an early deep-learning platform for comprehensive ECG annotation evolved into a broader operational capability for ambulatory cardiac monitoring. The talk highlights the informatics challenges behind adoption, including dataset design, annotation quality, edge-case discovery, validation strategy, workflow integration, human oversight, and model evolution after deployment.

The session offers a pragmatic roadmap for organizations that want to move from impressive algorithms to human-centered AI systems that are safe, scalable, and genuinely useful in healthcare.

Ben Teplitzky, PhD

AI/ML Research Fellow, Patient Management

Boston Scientific, USA
