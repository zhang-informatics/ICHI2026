8:15 AM - 8:35 AM Policy Keynote
Policy Keynote Theater

Evaluating Large Language Models for Clinical Decision Support

A policy-focused keynote on AI metrology, trustworthiness, and rigorous evaluation standards for clinical LLMs.

Abstract

Large language models are increasingly being used to support clinicians by analyzing unstructured text, medical literature, and clinical guidance. This keynote examines the growing need for rigorous AI metrology in healthcare, focusing on standardized methods to assess the accuracy, reliability, and trustworthiness of LLMs.

The talk also reviews emerging evaluation metrics for healthcare chatbots from an end-user perspective, including language understanding, performance on real-world clinical tasks, conversational effectiveness, and the specific measurement needs introduced by agentic AI architectures.

Session Snapshot

  • Examines how clinical LLMs can be evaluated for accuracy, reliability, and trust.
  • Highlights AI metrology as a practical foundation for healthcare deployment.
  • Connects evaluation design to real-world clinical decision support settings.

8:35 AM - 9:45 AM Policy Panel
Policy Panel Theater

From Data to Policy: Governing AI and Health Informatics in the Age of Intelligent Care

A cross-sector discussion on accountability, privacy, equity, and governance for health AI and real-world data.

Overview

The rapid integration of AI, large-scale health data systems, and interoperable informatics platforms into clinical and public health settings has outpaced the policy frameworks designed to govern them.

This panel brings together leaders from government, standards, clinical research, and health policy to examine where innovation meets regulation, including AI accountability in clinical decision support, data privacy under evolving federal and state frameworks, health equity in algorithmic systems, and governance of real-world data for drug and device evaluation.

Discussion Themes

  • Policy pathways that protect patients without stalling innovation
  • Governance of clinical AI and real-world data infrastructures
  • How informatics researchers can shape the regulatory landscape

10:15 AM - 11:45 AM Panel Discussion
Panel Discussion Theater

The GenAI Data Chasm: Are We Building a Human-Aligned Unified Data Ecosystem or Just Deeper, More Intelligent Silos?

Success stories, technical realities, and the infrastructure gaps that shape whether healthcare AI scales safely.

Overview

This panel focuses on one of the biggest barriers to successful AI adoption in healthcare: the gap between simply having data and having data that is trustworthy, representative, and fit for purpose in a medical-grade AI ecosystem.

Panelists will examine whether the field is moving toward safer, more unified, human-aligned data ecosystems or creating deeper and more intelligent silos that trap value, amplify bias, and weaken real-world impact for patients, providers, and innovators.

Discussion Focus

  • Trustworthy, representative, and fit-for-purpose real-world data
  • Unified infrastructure versus fragmented AI silos
  • Bias, safety, interoperability, and long-term scalability

11:45 AM - 12:45 PM Conference Keynote
Conference Keynote Theater

Towards Virtual Patient: AI for Accelerating Medical Discovery

A conference keynote on multimodal generative AI, digital twins, and virtual patient models for real-world evidence.

Abstract

Today, medical discovery advances one clinical trial at a time, each taking years to execute and often costing vast amounts of time and money. This keynote explores how multimodal generative AI can learn the language of patients from longitudinal EHR, imaging, and multi-omics data to create virtual patient world models.

Such digital twins can synthesize population-scale real-world evidence from hundreds of millions of patients and help accelerate discovery through AI-powered virtual clinical trials.

Session Snapshot

  • Focuses on virtual patient models built from multimodal longitudinal data.
  • Brings together EHR, imaging, and multi-omics perspectives in one keynote.
  • Explores how digital twins may accelerate discovery through virtual trials.

Lessons from real-world deployment across health systems, medtech, and research collaborations.

3:30 PM - 5:00 PM Industry Keynote
Industry Keynote Theater

From Algorithm to Adoption: Evolving BeatLogic into Trusted Real-World AI for Cardiac Monitoring

A practical keynote on what it takes to move from a strong model to a trusted operational AI capability in healthcare.

Abstract

Artificial intelligence in healthcare is often presented through model performance, but real-world impact depends on much more than accuracy alone. Using BeatLogic as a case study, this keynote examines the transition from an impressive algorithm to a trusted operational AI capability in ambulatory cardiac monitoring.

The talk highlights dataset design, annotation quality, edge-case discovery, validation strategy, workflow integration, human oversight, and responsible model evolution after deployment.

Session Snapshot

  • Uses BeatLogic as a case study for translating AI into operational practice.
  • Centers on validation, workflow fit, human oversight, and post-deployment evolution.
  • Frames adoption as an informatics and trust challenge, not only a modeling challenge.
